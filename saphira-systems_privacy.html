02What we collect

We process the following categories of personal data:

Contact form & inquiry data. When you use the contact form, send us an email, or reach out via WhatsApp / LinkedIn, we receive:

  • Name, company, country
  • Email address, phone number (if you provide it)
  • Type of interest (partnership, project, government / defense, EPC, technical, media)
  • Optional context: region / market, estimated project size, application area, preferred contact method
  • The content of your message
  • Technical metadata of the submission (timestamp, browser user agent, referring page)

Server log data. Our hosting provider automatically stores standard web-server logs:

  • IP address (anonymised where supported)
  • Date and time of access
  • URL accessed, HTTP status, transferred data volume
  • User agent, referrer

These logs are kept for a maximum of 30 days to ensure operational stability and protect against abuse.

Cookies / tracking. Strictly-necessary technical cookies (e.g. remembering your language preference if you set one) are always active. We use the HubSpot tracking code (provided by HubSpot Ireland Limited, EU data centre) to understand how our site is used and to qualify B2B leads; it is active when you visit the site, on the legal basis of our legitimate interest in operating and improving a B2B sales website (Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR, see section 03). You can object to this processing at any time (see section 06). A full breakdown of this tool — provider, purpose, storage, third-country safeguards and your right to object — is set out in section 08 of our Imprint. In addition, only with your explicit consent via our opt-in cookie banner, we use Google Analytics 4 with IP anonymisation; it loads only after you click “Accept” and you can withdraw that consent at any time. We do not use advertising or social-tracking cookies. Embedded third-party links (LinkedIn, WhatsApp) only load when you click them.

03Why we process it (legal basis)

  • Art. 6 (1) (b) GDPR — to take pre-contractual steps at your request (responding to a project, partnership, or partnership inquiry).
  • Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR — our legitimate interest in operating a secure, functional website and qualifying inbound leads for our B2B sales process. Your fundamental rights remain protected and you may object at any time (see section 06).
  • Art. 6 (1) (a) GDPR — your explicit consent (privacy checkbox in the contact form). You may withdraw consent at any time, effective for the future.
  • Art. 6 (1) (c) GDPR — where we are required by law to retain or share data (e.g. tax, accounting obligations).

04Who receives your data

We share personal data only where necessary and only with carefully-selected processors bound by GDPR-compliant data-processing agreements (Art. 28 GDPR):

  • Email and office providers — for sending and receiving the inquiry response.
  • Hosting provider — for serving this website.
  • CRM / sales & analytics tooling — HubSpot Ireland Limited (EU data centre; parent HubSpot, Inc., USA), to track and respond to your inquiry and to measure website usage.
  • Professional advisors — legal, tax, or accounting partners under professional confidentiality, only when required.

We do not sell personal data and do not use it for automated decision-making or profiling.

Transfers outside the EU. Where a processor is located in a third country (e.g. United States), we rely on adequacy decisions of the EU Commission or, where unavailable, EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) under Art. 46 GDPR with additional safeguards as required by case law. For HubSpot specifically, data is primarily processed in its EU data centre; any transfer to the US parent company is safeguarded by the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework and, in addition, by EU Standard Contractual Clauses.

05How long we keep it

  • Inquiry data — kept as long as the lead is active and for up to 24 months after the last interaction, so we can pick up the conversation if you come back. After that, the data is deleted or anonymised, unless we are required to retain it for legal reasons.
  • Contract / commercial data — retained for the statutory retention period of 7 years under § 132 BAO (Austrian Federal Fiscal Code).
  • Server logs — 30 days, then deleted.
  • Consent records — kept for as long as the underlying processing continues, plus 3 years for evidence purposes.

06Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights with regard to your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — to know what data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — to have your data deleted, subject to legal retention periods.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18) — to limit how we process your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — to processing based on legitimate interest, at any time.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7) — effective for the future, where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these rights, write to info@saphira-systems.at. We respond within one month.
